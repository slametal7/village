Hammond, Wisconsin

Hammond is a village in St. Croix County, Wisconsin, United States. The population was 1,922 at the 2010 census. The village is within the Town of Hammond. Hammond was home to the Running of the Llamas.





== History ==

Hammond was named in 1856 for R. B. Hammond, an original owner of the town site. A post office called Hammond has been in operation since 1858.





== Geography ==

According to the United States Census Bureau, the village has an area of 2.15 square miles (5.57 km2), all land.

Hammond is located at 44°58′32″N 92°26′7″W (44.975598, -92.435452).

Hammond is along U.S. Highway 12.
